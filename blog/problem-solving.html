A Time I Was Blocked on a Simple Problem

The Problem:

I remember a frustrating day when I was trying to figure out a bug in my code that was supposed to sort a list of names alphabetically. It seemed like a simple task, but I kept getting unexpected results. Names were appearing in the wrong order, and I couldn’t pinpoint why.

Problem-Solving Techniques:

To tackle this issue, I tried breaking the problem down into smaller parts. First, I wrote pseudocode to outline the steps I thought my sorting algorithm should follow. Then, I used console.logging to print out the intermediate results at various stages of my function. I also Googled similar sorting algorithms to see if I could identify what I was missing.

How I Felt:

Initially, I felt overwhelmed and a bit defeated, especially when nothing seemed to work. After a few hours of trial and error, I started feeling a little more optimistic as I implemented each change and saw small improvements. Eventually, when I found the error, I felt a rush of relief and satisfaction.

What I learned:

This experience taught me the importance of patience and the value of breaking down problems. I realized that even simple problems can become complex and that systematic troubleshooting is often the key to finding a solution.

A Time I Elegantly Solved a Problem

The Problem:

Another time, I was tasked with creating a simple web form for user feedback. I wanted to ensure that all user inputs were validated before submission. Instead of writing complex validation rules for each field, I aimed for a more elegant solution.

Problem-Solving Techniques

I decided to utilize regular expressions for validating inputs. I started by drafting pseudocode that laid out my validation logic clearly. I also wrote a simple helper function that checked the validity of the inputs and returned error messages if necessary. This made my code more reusable and cleaner.

How I Felt

The process felt smooth and enjoyable. I was in a flow state as I saw my ideas come together. When I finally tested the form and everything worked seamlessly, I felt accomplished and proud of my clean solution.

What I Learned

This situation taught me that sometimes taking a step back and thinking critically about a problem can lead to elegant and efficient solutions. It reinforced my belief in the power of planning before diving into coding.

Reflecting on Problem-Solving Techniques

1. Pseudocode:

I feel quite confident using pseudocode. It helps me outline my thoughts and plan my approach without getting lost in syntax.

2. Trying Something:

I’m generally comfortable with trying different solutions, although it can be intimidating when I’m unsure.

3. Rubber Ducky Method

This method I have not used at all as I felt awkward talking to an inanimate object. I might as well give it a go and reflect back on it in my future reflections

4. Reading Error Messages:

I’ve become better at interpreting error messages over time, though they still occasionally confuse me.

5. Console.logging:

Console.logging is one of my go-to techniques. It’s straightforward and gives immediate feedback.

6. Googling

I rely heavily on Googling. It feels empowering to find solutions online, and I’ve learned to use specific keywords for better results.

7. Asking Peers for Help:

I’m somewhat hesitant to ask peers for help, as I don’t want to feel like a burden. However, I recognize the value of collaboration.

8. Asking Coaches for Help:

I feel more comfortable asking coaches for help, as they were nothing but helpful and very supportive

9. Improving with Reflection:

Reflecting on my process has become a valuable tool for me. I often review what worked and what didn’t to improve future problem-solving.
